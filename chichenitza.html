Chichen Itza

  1. Chichen Itza is thought to be from around the year 600 AD.

    2. Archaeologists believe Chichen Itza to have been once a powerful economic city developed around the year 600AD and believe the fall of the city to be approximately 1000 AD.

  2. Founded by the Maya, an ancient people native to the Yucatan peninsula, Mexico.

    3. Chichen Itza was founded by the Maya, an ancient people native to the Yucatan peninsula and covers 4 square mile (10 square km). It is believed 35,000 people resided in the city. It is thought the areas accessibility to a natural water source via the caves and sinkholes drew the first settlers to the site.

  3. El Castillo is the most recognisable structure of Chichen Itza.

    4. The pyramids and temples were designed in clusters and the most famous and largest pyramid El Castillo (the temple of Kukullken) is one of the most visited sites at Chichen Itza and actually sits on top of another much larger temple. The temple has 365 steps representing each day of the year. These steps are divided in 91 steps on each of the four sides of the structure and the last step to the top is the 365th.

  4. Chichen Itza is located near the small town of Piste, Mexico.

    5. Located near the small town of Piste, Chichen Itza sees around two million tourists each year who flock to see the ancient city. The most popular time of year is the spring and autumn equinox as this is when visitors can see the shadow serpent of the El Castillo.
